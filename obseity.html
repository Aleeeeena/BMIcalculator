WELLNESSGAUGE

Your health, your numbers, your choice

Eat nutrient-dense foods:

Focus on whole, minimally processed foods like fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains, and healthy fats. Avoid excessive consumption of sugary, fried, or highly processed foods that are high in empty calories.

Stay active

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise (like brisk walking) or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise (like running) per week. Include strength training exercises at least twice a week to build muscle and boost metabolism.

Mindful eating

Pay attention to hunger and fullness cues to avoid overeating. Avoid eating in front of the TV or while distracted, as it can lead to mindless eating and larger portion sizes.

Drink water regularly

Sometimes thirst can be mistaken for hunger, leading to overeating. Drinking water before meals can also help control appetite and prevent overeating.

Avoid emotional eating

Be mindful of emotional triggers that may lead to overeating. Find healthier ways to cope with stress, such as physical activity or talking to a friend or professional.

Practice stress-reducing activities:

Chronic stress can lead to emotional eating and cravings for unhealthy foods. Try stress management techniques such as yoga, meditation, deep breathing, or engaging in hobbies that help you relax.
